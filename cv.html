my PhD research

For my PhD thesis, Kinship terminology adapts for efficient communication at multiple levels of linguistic structure, I am using a combination of computational models, behavioural experiments, and phylogenetic analyses to study:

  • common patterns in how family relationships are categorised across the world's languages,
  • how these patterns support efficient communication at the structural and semantic level,
  • and how the design of semantic category systems reflects an adaptation to the cultural evolution of language.
To learn more, have a look at my ongoing research projects.
